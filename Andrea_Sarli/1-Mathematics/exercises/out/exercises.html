Scalar Multiplication α⋅(x,y)=(αx,αy)\alpha \cdot (x, y) = (\alpha x, \alpha y)α⋅(x,y)=(αx,αy)

Now we verify that these operations satisfy the 8 axioms of a vector space over R\mathbb{R}R:

  1. Closure under addition:
    If (x1,y1),(x2,y2)∈R2(x_1, y_1), (x_2, y_2) \in \mathbb{R}^2(x1​,y1​),(x2​,y2​)∈R2, then (x1+x2,y1+y2)∈R2(x_1 + x_2, y_1 + y_2) \in \mathbb{R}^2(x1​+x2​,y1​+y2​)∈R2. By definition of R2\mathbb{R}^2R2, we know that x1,x2,y1,y2∈Rx_1, x_2, y_1, y_2 \in \mathbb{R}x1​,x2​,y1​,y2​∈R.

    We define vector addition as:

    (x1,y1)+(x2,y2)=(x1+x2,y1+y2)(x_1, y_1) + (x_2, y_2) = (x_1 + x_2, y_1 + y_2) (x1​,y1​)+(x2​,y2​)=(x1​+x2​,y1​+y2​)

  6. Compatibility of scalar multiplication with real multiplication:
    (αβ)⋅(x,y)=α⋅(β⋅(x,y))(\alpha \beta) \cdot (x, y) = \alpha \cdot (\beta \cdot (x, y))(αβ)⋅(x,y)=α⋅(β⋅(x,y))

2. Verifying that ∥x∥2\|x\|_2∥x∥2​ is a norm:
